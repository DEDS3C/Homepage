CCENT and CCNA Certification

The role and skills required of network engineers are evolving and are more vital than ever. The Cisco Certified Network Associate (CCNA) Routing and Switching certification demonstrates you have a knowledge of foundational technologies and ensures you stay relevant with skill sets needed for the adoption of next generation technologies.

The CCNA Routing and Switching certification is achieved by passing two exams or one composite exam. A candidate who passes the first exam is qualified to be called a Cisco Certified Entry Network Technician (CCENT). The CCENT exam tests a candidate's knowledge and skills related to network fundamentals, LAN switching technologies, routing technologies, infrastructure services, and infrastructure maintenance. The second CCNA exam tests a candidate's knowledge and skills related to IPv4 and IPv6 routing technologies and WAN technologies, as well as more advanced technologies in LAN switching, infrastructure services, and infrastructure maintenance.

This course helps set the stage for networking concepts and basic routing and switching configurations and is a start on your path towards CCENT and CCNA certification.
