Basic Router Configuration Steps

Similar to configuring a switch, the tasks listed in Figure 1 should be completed when configuring initial settings on a router.

Figures 2 through 5 provide an example of these tasks being configured on a router. In Figure 2, the router is assigned a hostname. In Figure 3, the privileged EXEC, user EXEC, and remote access lines are secured with a password and all passwords in the configuration file are encrypted. Providing legal notification is configured in Figure 4. And finally, the configuration is being saved in Figure 5.

Use the Syntax Checker in Figure 6 to practice these configuration steps.

Figure 1 shows the steps to configure a router with its initial settings including: configure the device name; configure passwords for privilege mode and for the lines; configure a banner; configure the SVI; and save the configuration. Figures 2 through 5 show a two router topology. Each router has two switches attached. Figure 2 shows an example of configuring the hostname command. Figure 3 shows an example of configuring the passwords. Figure 4 shows an example of configuring the banner. Figure 5 shows an example of saving the configuration. Figure 6 is a Syntax Checker allowing the learner to practice configuring a router.
