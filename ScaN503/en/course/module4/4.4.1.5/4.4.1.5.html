Configuring Wireless Settings

After establishing the connection to a router, it is good practice to configure some basic settings to help secure the wireless network:

  • Network Mode – Some wireless routers allow you to select which 802.11 standard to implement. Figure 1 shows that “Mixed” has been selected. This means wireless devices connecting to the wireless router can have a variety of wireless NICs installed including 802.11a, b, g, and n.

  • Network Name (SSID) – Assign an SSID to the wireless network. Home-Net is used in Figure 1. If the SSID broadcast is disabled, you must manually enter the SSID on wireless devices.

  • Standard Channel - The 802.11b and 802.11g standards commonly use channels 1, 6, and 11 to avoid interference. In Figure 1, the channel is manually set to 6.

  • Wireless Security - Configure the strongest security mode, which is WPA2 with AES encryption, as shown in Figure 2.
